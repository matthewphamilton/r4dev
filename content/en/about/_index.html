The goal of ready4 is to be a valid and useful youth mental health systems model.

{{< /blocks/cover >}} {{% blocks/lead %}}

ready4 was catalysed in July 2017 with funding from VicHealth, Orygen and Victoria University to develop an open source simulation model of resilience in young people. At that time, the project was called readyforwhatsnext.

{{% /blocks/lead %}} {{< blocks/section >}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-comment" title="Stakeholder Engagement" %}} We got [advice and feedback](/docs/contribution-guidelines/contribution-types/advisory/) from young people, policy professionals, [modellers](/docs/getting-started/users/modeller/) and other researchers. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-map-marked" title="Conceptual Model" %}} We developed a [conceptual model](/blog/2021/02/12/readyforwhatsnext-conference-posters-now-available/) to inform the development of the computational model. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-check" title="Prototype" %}} We authored [development release software](/docs/getting-started/software/status/development-releases/) to support implementation of the ready4 [framework](/docs/framework/) and [model](/docs/model/). {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{< /blocks/section >}} {{% blocks/lead %}}

The current phase of development of ready4 is focused on developing a Minimum Viable Product of the ready4 model and is being progressed by a PhD project at Monash University's Health Economics Group and multiple computational modelling projects at Orygen's Health Services and Outcomes Research team.

{{% /blocks/lead %}} {{< blocks/section >}} {{< cardpane >}} {{< card header="PhD" footer="Primary Supervisor: Cathy Mihalopoulos">}} The PhD candidature of ready4's project lead Matthew Hamilton is focused on developing ready4 as a valid and user-friendly tool to inform policymaking and system design relating to primary mental health services for young people in Australia. In addition to peer-reviewed publications, targeted outputs from this PhD program of research include production releases of ready4 framework libraries and module libraries, open access datasets, reproducible analyses and decision aids. {{< /card >}} {{< card header="Modelling projects" footer="Head of Unit: Sue Cotton">}} ready4 is being applied to a number of computational modelling projects at Orygen, that extend the scope of ready4 beyond what is being developed under the PhD program. Examples of current and recent projects include those in choice modelling and utility mapping. Details of project funders are available in the citation information, meta-data and acknowledgements of the modules, datasets, analyses and manuscripts associated with each project. {{< /card >}} {{< /cardpane >}} {{< /blocks/section >}} {{< blocks/section >}}

All ready4 code to date has been written by Matthew Hamilton, Glenn Weisner and Caroline Gao. Our planned next phase of development will therefore focus on building a community to sustain, extend and apply ready4 - and we need your help to make this happen!
