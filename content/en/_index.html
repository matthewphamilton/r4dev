{{< /blocks/cover >}} {{% blocks/lead color="primary" %}} readyforwhatsnext is an open source project to develop and combine reference models of the systems that shape the mental health and wellbeing of young people. {{% /blocks/lead %}} {{< blocks/section >}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fab fa-app-store-ios" title="People" %}} Modelling the characteristics, relationships, behaviours, risk factors and outcomes of children and young people. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fab fa-github" title="Places" url="https://github.com/google/docsy-example" %}} Modelling the role of area and proximity characteristics on outcomes, exploring geographic equity {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fab fa-twitter" title="Platforms" url="https://twitter.com/mph_economist" %}} Modelling the accessibility, efficient configuration and resourcing requirements of health services for young people. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fab fa-twitter" title="Programs" url="https://twitter.com/mph_economist" %}} Modelling the affordability, value for money and appropriate targeting of interventions to improve mental health and wellbeing outcomes in young people. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{< /blocks/section >}} {{< blocks/section >}}

We provide three categories of open source tools.

{{< /blocks/section >}} {{< blocks/section >}} {{< cardpane >}} {{< card header="Models" >}} Each self-contained modelling project is a module. Modules can be combined to model more extensive systems. {{< /card >}} {{< card header="Data" >}} Models are fed data, specific to the decision context. All non-confidential data are publicly available. {{< /card >}} {{< card header="Analyses" >}} When models are supplied with data, they generate insight to help improve the lives of young people. {{< /card >}} {{< /cardpane >}} {{< /blocks/section >}} {{< blocks/section >}}

As an open source project, we welcome your contributions!

{{< /blocks/section >}} {{< blocks/section >}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fab fa-app-store-ios" title="Young people" %}} Young people - participate in our research and help shape our priorities. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fab fa-app-store-ios" title="Developers" url="https://github.com/google/docsy-example" %}} R and Python developers - we'd welcome your help to improve the quality and usefulness of our computational models. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fab fa-github" title="Researchers" %}} Economic, mental health and child and youth researchers - it would be great to see you use our work in your open science projects. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fab fa-twitter" title="Planners" url="https://twitter.com/mph_economist" %}} Policymakers and mental health service planners - apply our toolkits and make your data more accessible. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fab fa-twitter" title="Funders" url="https://twitter.com/mph_economist" %}} Commissioners, research grant bodies and sponsors - accelerate our development, so young people benefit from better decision making. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{< /blocks/section >}}
